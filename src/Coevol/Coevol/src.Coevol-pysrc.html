  10  """ 
  11      Detects intra and inter molecular coevolving sites and proteins 
  12       
  13      Program runs in three different mode - Inter, Intra and Mega 
  14       
  15       
  16                                          Mega mode 
  17       
  18      In Mega mode program calculates pairwise distance between amino acid/cds sequences  
  19      of all the taxon present in the input dataset and performs pearson correlation 
  20      analysis to detect coevolution statistics. 
  21       
  22       
  23       
  24      Based on 
  25       
  26      Hua Zhou, Eric Jakobsson (2013) 
  27      Predicting Protein-Protein Interaction by the Mirrortree Method: Possibilities and Limitations. 
  28      PLoS One 8(12): e81100. 
  29       
  30       
  31       
  32                                      Inter and Intra mode 
  33       
  34      In Intra and Inter mode, program estimates site coevolution statistics using CAPS method. 
  35      Program also allows user to choose between standard pearson correlation coefficient method 
  36      implimented in CAPS program and Gobels site correlation test algorithm.  
  37       
  38      Program allows user to restrict the execution to the selected list of alignment positions 
  39      to reduce the execution runtime in the cases where the user wants to test correlation 
  40      between specific alignment sites. 
  41       
  42      It can also detect the presence/absence of interatomic interactions, such as the hydrogen bonds, 
  43      at the coevolving sites. 
  44       
  45       
  46       
  47      Based upon  
  48       
  49      'CAPS: coevolution analysis using protein sequences' 
  50      Fares MA, McNally D. Bioinformatics. 2006 Nov 15;22(22):2821-2. 
  51       
  52      Gobel U, Sander C, Schneider R and Valencia A. (1994) Proteins, 18, 309-317. 
  53       
  54      Manish C.Saraf, Gregory L.Moore and Costas D.Maranas. 
  55      Using multiple sequence correlation analysis to characterize functionally important protein regions. 
  56      Protein Engineering vol. 16 no. 6 pp. 397-406, 2003 
  57       
  58       
  59       
  60       
  61                  "Site Correlation" - Inter and Intra Mode 
  62       
  63      BLOSUM values are normalized by the time of divergence between 
  64      sequences. BLOSUM values (Bek) are thus weighted for the transition  
  65      between amino acids e and k using the time (t) since the divergence  
  66      between sequences i and j: 
  67       
  68                        θ_ek(ij) = Bek*(t^-1)(ij) 
  69       
  70      The assumption made in equation 1 is that the different types of amino  
  71      acid transitions (slight to radical amino acid changes) in a particular  
  72      site follow a Poisson distribution along time. The greater the time since  
  73      the divergence between sequences i and j the greater the probability of  
  74      having a radical change. A linear relationship is thus assumed between the  
  75      BLOSUM values and time. Synonymous substitutions per site (dSij) are silent  
  76      mutations, as they do not affect the amino acid composition of the protein.  
  77      These mutations are therefore neutrally fixed in the gene. Assuming that  
  78      synonymous sites are not saturated or under constraints, dS is proportional  
  79      to the time since the two sequences compared diverged. Time (t) therefore is  
  80      measured as dS. 
  81       
  82      The next step is the estimation of the mean θ parameter for each site (θC~)  
  83      of the alignment, so that: 
  84       
  85                     θC~ = (1/T)sigma[1->T]θ_ek(S) 
  86       
  87      Here S refers to each pairwise comparison, while T stands for the total number 
  88      of pairwise sequence comparisons, and thus: 
  89       
  90                            T = N*(N-1)/2 
  91       
  92      Where N is the total number of sequences in the alignment. 
  93       
  94      The variability of each pairwise amino acid transition compared to that of  
  95      the site column is estimated as: 
  96       
  97                      Dˆ_ek = (θ_ek(ij) - θC~)^2 
  98       
  99      The mean variability for the corrected BLOSUM transition values is: 
 100       
 101                 DC= (1/T)*sigma[1->T](θ_ek(S)−θC~)2 
 102       
 103      The coevolution between amino acid sites (A and B) is estimated thereafter by  
 104      measuring the correlation in the pairwise amino acid variability, relative to  
 105      the mean pairwise variability per site, between them. This covariation is  
 106      measured as the correlation between their Dˆ ek values.  
 107       
 108      The mean correlation coefficient and its variance are then estimated. Correlation  
 109      coefficients are then tested for significance under a normal distribution. 
 110       
 111      The statistical power of the test is optimised by analysing sites showing: 
 112       
 113                           DC > phi − 2σ_phi 
 114       
 115      Here, phi is the parametric value of DC and σ is the standard deviation of phi. 
 116       
 117      Pair-wise comparisons including gaps in any or both sites at any sequence are  
 118      excluded from the analysis. 
 119
